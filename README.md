# CS-230-final

# Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?

My client was a company called The Gaming Room. They wanted to expand on their already popular game, Draw It or Lose It, by turning it from a mobile only. To a web-based cross-platform application. This meant that the original game that was made for Android devices needed to be changed and updated so that it could be scalable and distirbuted to support multiple teams, players, and sessions across multiple computers and operating systems.

# What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?

For my end of the assignment. I believe I did an excellent job at identifying the design choice. Comparing the three main OS's with each other and determining which would be the most optimal was something I enjoyed doing. I felt as if my choice was backed by solid reasoning as well. 

# What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?

Working through a design has always helped me in writing actual code. The planning phase of programming can be debatably even more important than any other step. Knowing what you want your code to do before you start writing can help the process be smooth and help identify bugs should they show. It also helps with troubleshooting as if you are able to know what your work should be doing. You can more easily determine why it is not doing it.

# How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

Following the business requirements. My main goal for the user's needs were simple for this assignment. The game already existed and was extremely popular. So, carrying over all those features that users had come to enjoy over the course of their time with the game. As well as add on new features on the PC version. Taking user needs into consideration is extremely important as it can change the entire landscape of the application based on if features are needed or not. 

# How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?

My personal approach was to plan, think, program, try. I planned out everything I wanted to do for the project. From business requirements to user's needs. Then I think on how those features and plans can be translated to the program. Mostly done through charts and kanban boards. Following this, I finally begin to program those ideas before trying them out in intervals. I feel this process works for me because it keeps you in a loop of not coding too much first and then running into a wall of errors.
